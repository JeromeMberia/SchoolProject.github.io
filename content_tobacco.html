TOBACCO

What is the problem with tobacco abuse?

Smoking causes cancer, heart disease, stroke, lung diseases, diabetes, and chronic obstructive
pulmonary disease (COPD), which includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis. Smoking also increases
risk for tuberculosis, certain eye diseases, and problems of the immune system,
including rheumatoid arthritis

Facts about using tobacco

  1. Tobacco is a killer.
  2. It increases your chance of dying.
  4. Smoking 1 cigarette can take 11 minutes off your life.
  5. Smoking is a serious cause of illness.
  6. Smoking affects fertility.
  7. It gives you bad breath.
signs of necotine addiction
  1. Cravings or feeling like you really need to use tobacco.
  2. Going out of your way to get tobacco.
  3. Feeling anxious or irritable if you want to use tobacco but can't.
How long does it take to get rid of tobacco addiction?

Once the body adapts to regular nicotine intake, people find giving up smoking difficult because of the uncomfortable
symptoms of nicotine withdrawal. Withdrawal symptoms usually
peak after 1-3 days and then decrease over a period of 3-4 weeks.
